CONTRACT OF EMPLOYMENT
Trilogy CX (Pty) Ltd (Reg. No. 2024/457320/07) t/a Trilogy BPO — hereinafter "the Company"
and the Employee whose details appear in Schedule A hereto
Entered into between:
Trilogy CX (Pty) Ltd (Registration Number: 2024/457320/07), hereinafter referred to as the "Company"

And: —, hereinafter referred to as the "Employee"
1. Appointment & Position

The Company appoints the Employee in the position and campaign/project as set out in Schedule A with effect from the commencement date set out in Schedule A.

The Employee shall perform the functions associated with and ancillary to the position set out in Schedule A, and any other functions that the Company may reasonably request from time to time.

The Employee's formal place of work will be at the Company's premises stipulated in Schedule A or at such other premises as the Company may occupy or relocate to from time to time.

2. Termination, Period of Employment and Suspension

Either party shall be entitled to terminate this agreement on written notice: one week if employed less than 3 months; two weeks if employed for 3–6 months; one month if employed more than 6 months.

The Company shall be entitled to terminate the Employee's employment summarily if the Employee is guilty of conduct justifying summary dismissal, convicted of fraud/dishonesty/theft, fails to perform duties satisfactorily, or for any other reason justified in law.

The Company may suspend the Employee pending investigation into alleged misconduct for a period not exceeding 30 days, during which the Employee shall be entitled to their salary.

3. Duties of Employee

The Employee shall satisfactorily and lawfully perform all duties reasonably assigned, devote their full time and attention during hours of work, obey orders and directions of the Company, use company assets with utmost care, and maintain confidentiality of all company records and affairs.

The Employee shall not exceed the express limits of authority attendant to their position, shall use utmost endeavours to protect and promote the business interests of the Company, and shall disclose any potential conflicts of interest at the earliest opportunity.

4. Remuneration

As remuneration for the Employee's services, the Company shall pay the Employee the remuneration package as set out in Schedule A. The Employee's gross monthly salary shall be paid by electronic funds transfer into the Employee's bank account before the last day of each month.

The Company shall review the Employee's remuneration package from time to time and it shall be within the sole and absolute discretion of the Company whether or not to grant any increase.

5. Medical Aid, Provident Fund & Group Life Cover

The Company does not currently offer a medical aid scheme, provident fund or group life cover. The Employee is made aware that the costs for any such schemes are for the sole cost of the Employee.

6. Hours of Work

The Employee will be required to work the minimum number of hours during the days and times set out in Schedule A. Hours may vary depending on operational requirements. The Employee may also be required to work overtime, nightshift and/or over weekends if and when required by the Company.

7. Annual Leave

The Employee will be entitled to 15 working days leave on full pay per completed year of service as set out in Schedule A. Leave must be taken within 6 months after completion of the annual leave cycle in which it accrues, on dates agreed by both parties as mutually convenient.

8. Sick Leave

The Employee is entitled to sick leave in accordance with the provisions of the Basic Conditions of Employment Act 75 of 1997. The Employee must inform the Company immediately upon absence due to illness and must provide a medical certificate for absences exceeding two consecutive days or more than two occasions in an eight-week period.

9. Consent to Credit and Criminal Checks & POPIA

By signing this agreement, the Employee consents to the Company accessing their credit profile, conducting fraud and criminal checks, and furnishing personal information to suppliers and professional advisors who render services to the Company, in accordance with the Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013.

10. Confidentiality

The Employee undertakes not to disclose confidential information to any third party, to use utmost endeavours to maintain confidentiality, and to return all confidential information upon demand. These obligations shall survive termination of employment.

11. Incompatible Activities and Non-Solicitation

The Employee shall not, without the Company's prior written consent, be engaged in any business other than that of the Company. For the duration of employment and 12 months thereafter, the Employee shall not solicit or induce any Company employee to leave or join a competing business.

12. Restraint of Trade

The Employee undertakes, while employed and for the restraint period set out in Schedule A (6 months after termination), not to carry on or be engaged in any business competitive with the Company's business in the Republic of South Africa, nor to solicit clients, customers, suppliers or employees of the Company.

13. Intellectual Property

The Employee shall immediately disclose all discoveries, inventions, improvements and designs made in the course of employment, all of which shall belong to and be the sole property of the Company. The Employee cedes and assigns all copyright and intellectual property rights to the Company. These obligations continue after termination of employment.

14. Company Property and Communications

The Company's IT systems and all communications made using them are the property of the Company. The Company is entitled to monitor, access and intercept the Employee's communications. IT systems must only be used for purposes of conducting the Company's business.

15. General

This document constitutes the sole record of the agreement between the parties. No addition, variation or cancellation shall be of force or effect unless in writing and signed by both parties. This agreement may be signed in counterparts, including electronic form.

16. Addresses
